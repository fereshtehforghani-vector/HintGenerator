Class 2: Introduction to Microcontrollers

Imagine a tiny computer, smaller than your palm, quietly running behind the scenes in many devices you use every day. This small computer, known as a microcontroller, acts as the brain that controls specific tasks within electronics.

Unlike the big computers you use at school or home, a microcontroller is designed to do **one job really well**. It’s found inside devices like TV remotes, video game controllers, microwaves, washing machines, and even in robots!

### **What’s Inside a Microcontroller?**

A microcontroller has several important parts packed into one chip:


- 

**CPU (Central Processing Unit):** This is the “brain” of the microcontroller. It performs all the thinking and calculations.


- 

**Memory:** Just like a notepad, it temporarily stores essential information — like which button you pressed or sensor data it needs to remember.


- 

**Input/Output (I/O) Pins:** These are used to connect the microcontroller to buttons, LEDs, motors, sensors, and displays.


- 

**Timers, Counters, and Communication Ports:** These enable interaction with other devices and the management of time-based actions.



![S3 access](https://dp4rp4tcpuoo1.cloudfront.net/T720/Class1/c1.JPG)

### **What Does a Microcontroller Do?**

The microcontroller waits for input (like pressing a button), processes the input (thinks about what to do), and gives an output (like turning on an LED or starting a motor). It does all of this very quickly and automatically.

### **Real-Life Examples:**


- 

In a TV remote, the microcontroller reads your button press and sends the correct signal to the TV.


- 

In a microwave, it controls the timer, display, and heating based on your selection.


- 

A robot can read sensors and make decisions — like turning left when it detects an obstacle.


 

Microcontrollers may be small, but they’re incredibly powerful — they’re the hidden heroes that bring smart devices to life!

## Class 2: Meet the ZebraBot

<!-- type: lesson -->

Class 2: Meet the ZebraBot

### **Meet Z-Bot – The Brain Behind Your Robot!**

Say hello to Z-Bot, the powerful little controller that brings your robot to life! Just like your brain controls your body, Z-Bot controls every move your robot makes — from sensing the environment to powering motors.

At its core, Z-Bot is powered by a smart microchip called the ESP32, which helps your robot think, move, and respond to the world around it.

---


### **Smart Connections for Smarter Robots**

Z-Bot comes with special ports that make it easy to plug in a wide variety of sensors and motors:


- 

**I2C Sensor Ports** – Connect sensors that measure distance, colour, temperature, and more.


- 

**Analog & Digital Sensor Ports** – Use these to detect light, sound, touch, and other physical inputs.


- 

**4 Motor Ports** – Control smart DC motors that know how far and how fast they move, thanks to built-in encoders.


- 

**Servo Motor Support** – Plug in small 9g servo motors to control arms, grippers, or rotating parts. Some sensor ports even double as servo motor ports!



---


### **Why Z-Bot?**

With Z-Bot, building your robot becomes fun, hands-on, and educational. Whether you're programming movements or setting up sensors, Z-Bot makes it simple to understand how real robots work — perfect for beginner inventors and future engineers!

## Class 2: Robot Safety & Care Guidelines

<!-- type: lesson -->

Class 2: Robot Safety & Care Guidelines

Robots are amazing — but they’re also delicate! To keep our robot team in top shape, please follow these important rules:


- 

**Always carry your robot with both hands.** It helps prevent accidental drops and damage.


- 

**Never run your robot on a table without side barriers.** Robots can be unpredictable — we don’t want them diving off the edge!


- 

**Be gentle when plugging in motors and sensors.** Only handle the plastic connector at the end of the wire. Avoid pulling or yanking the wires.


- 

**Never pull a motor or sensor out by the cord.** Always insert and remove them by gripping the plastic connector. (Watch the demonstration video below for guidance.)


- 

**Avoid swinging motors or sensors by their wires.** Always hold them by their plastic casing to prevent internal damage.


- 

**Keep laptops on proper surfaces.** Never place them on the floor or against the walls of the robot challenge table. They should always rest on a flat desk or table.



By taking good care of your robot and tools, you'll ensure everything works smoothly — and your robot lives to complete another mission!
