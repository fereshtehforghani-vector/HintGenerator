Class 8: Introduction to 3D Designing

3D Design is the process of using software to mathematically represent any 3D Object. Various software and web-based tools and solutions are available for 3D Design. 

![image ](https://dp4rp4tcpuoo1.cloudfront.net/SDC/image5.1.0.png)![image ](https://dp4rp4tcpuoo1.cloudfront.net/SDC/image5.1.1.png)

What is the use of 3D Design?

3D designing is used in multiple sectors of the industry. The most common of them are as follows:


- Architecture Designing

- Automobile Industry

- Visual Effects and animation

- Virtual Reality and Virtual Environment

- Computer Aided Manufacturing

- Product Designing


Various 3D Design software applications are used for multiple purposes.

In this course, we will learn 3D Design using an online solution, **“TinkerCAD.”**

**Tools in TinkerCAD:**

List of tools and what they do in Tinkercad:

**Viewcube**: The cube at the top left of the software is known as the Viewcube. It helps you rotate the CAD model in all directions and gain different perspectives. 

**Home View**: The home view tool is the first of the five icons on the left of the work plane. The home view helps zoom out of the CAD model and gain an overall perspective of the model.

**Fit All In View**: The Fit All In View icon is underneath the Home View button. It helps you Zoom into a selected component of your CAD model and deal with it separately. 

**Zoom In**: The Zoom In button is the icon underneath the Fit All In view. It helps you zoom into the CAD model.

**Zoom Out**: The Zoom Out button is the icon underneath the Zoom In button. It helps you Zoom out of the CAD model. 

**Perspective View/Flat View**: This button is underneath the Zoom Out icon. It helps switch between Flat View and Perspective View in the CAD model. 

**Copy**: The Copy Tool is the top left tool in the toolbar above. It helps you copy components from the CAD model. 

**Paste**: The tool beside the Copy Tool is the Paste Tool. It helps you paste the copied component into the workplace.

**Duplicate**: The tool beside Paste is the Duplicate Tool. It automatically copies the component and moves it in a certain way, allowing you to duplicate it multiple times to create a pattern. 

**Delete**: The tool beside Duplicate is the Delete Tool. It helps you delete components you no longer need in your CAD design. 

**Undo and Redo buttons**: The Undo and Redo buttons help you redo previous changes or undo what you have already done in your CAD design. 

**Group**: The third tool on the right is the Group Tool. It helps you combine multiple components into one piece. 

**Ungroup**: The Ungroup tool is on the right of the Group Tool. It helps you break up grouped objects into multiple components. 

**Align Tool:** The Align tool aligns components on the x-axis, y-axis, and z-axis, grouping or lining them up into a CAD model.

**Mirror**: The Mirror tool is beside the Align tool. It helps you flip components in the opposite direction, either horizontally or vertically. 

**Import, Export, or Send to: **You can import files into your CAD Model to make assemblies, export the files to perform 3D printing or laser cutting, or send the CAD file to the person you want to send it to.

## Class 8: Design Your Car

Class 8: Design Your Car

This is your first step towards designing your car. When we talk about designing a car by ourselves, there are a few things that we need to take care of. Designing a vehicle does not mean designing a body; what matters the most is the support structure that holds the whole car together.

![image ](https://dp4rp4tcpuoo1.cloudfront.net/SDC/Image6.0.PNG)

That support structure is called the Chassis. The chassis is the main frame that holds everything into it to make a car work. There are other essential parts that we will be designing, but the most important parts are as follows:


1. Chassis

2. Steering Mechanism

3. Motor mounts

4. Wheel mounts

5. Circuit plate


We can not design all these parts one by one or simultaneously. We need to start with one and continue modifying it based on the other parts as we begin designing the chassis. Then, we start working on the steering mechanism and motor mounts and modify the chassis to make it compatible with them. We also need to keep assembling the parts to get an idea of the overall look of the car structure.

### **Steering Mechanism Overview**

After the chassis, the steering mechanism is the second most critical part of your robot car. It allows you to control direction and turning with precision.

In this class, you’ll be designing a 2-bar steering system, which is simple and highly effective — perfect for small robotic cars like yours.
 

#### **Types of Steering Mechanisms:**


1. 

**4-Bar (or 2-Bar) Steering System** – Common in RC cars and robotics due to its simplicity.


2. 

**Rack-and-Pinion System** – More complex, used in real cars for smoother control and precision.



### **How the 2-Bar Steering System Works**

**![image ](https://dp4rp4tcpuoo1.cloudfront.net/SDC/image6.6.0.png)**

Imagine a small parallelogram that turns the wheels:


- 

The **first bar** is a strong base that supports the wheels and servo. It's mounted to the chassis.


- 

The **second bar** (usually colored pink in diagrams) connects to the **servo motor**.


- 

When the servo turns, it **moves the second bar**, which rotates the wheels to steer the robot.
