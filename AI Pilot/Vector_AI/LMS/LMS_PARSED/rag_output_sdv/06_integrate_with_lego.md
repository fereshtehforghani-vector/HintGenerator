# Class 5 : Integrate with Lego
## Challenge #08: Integrate Chassis with motors

<!-- type: challenge -->

Assignment: Challenge #08: Integrate Chassis with motors

You’ve already built a solid LEGO chassis — the skeleton of your robot. Now that you’ve learned how **DC motors** provide drive and **servo motors** offer precise control, it’s time to bring your build to life by integrating both into your design.

### **Real-World Engineering in Action**

In this stage, you’ll use a DC motor connected to differential gears to drive your robot forward and backward. At the same time, a servo motor will control the steering mechanism, allowing your robot to turn left and right just like a real car.

This mimics how many real vehicles work — one motor drives the wheels, and another system steers them.

![image](https://dp4rp4tcpuoo1.cloudfront.net/SDV/image1.1.jpg)
 

![image](https://dp4rp4tcpuoo1.cloudfront.net/SDV/image1.5.jpg)
 

### **What You’ll Do:**


1. 

**Mount the DC Motor**
Install the DC motor to power the drivetrain. Using differential gears allows the wheels to rotate at different speeds, which is especially helpful when turning.


2. 

**Connect the Servo Motor**
Attach the servo motor to the steering system. This will allow you to control the angle of the front wheels with precision.


3. 

**Test Your Build**
Write and upload code to move your robot forward and steer it left/right. Adjust the gear ratios or motor positions if needed for smooth movement.



###  **What You’re Learning:**



- 

How to integrate motors into a chassis to build a functioning robot


- 

The difference between driving and steering systems


- 

Real-world applications of differential gears and servo-based steering


- 

Mechanical + electrical systems working together

## Challenge #10: Servo Steering in Action

<!-- type: challenge -->

Assignment: Challenge #10: Servo Steering in Action

Now that your **servo motor is connected to control steering**, it’s time to take your robot beyond just straight lines. This challenge focuses on **using the servo to turn your robot** accurately while the DC motor drives it.
 

### **Challenge: Make Your Robot Drive in a Circle!**

Your goal is to program the robot to drive in a smooth circle using your DC motor and servo motor together.

### ** Objectives:**


- 

Adjust the servo motor angle to steer the front wheels (or steering mechanism) in one direction.


- 

Keep the DC motor running at a constant speed to maintain motion.


- 

Fine-tune the servo angle until your robot completes a circular or curved path.
