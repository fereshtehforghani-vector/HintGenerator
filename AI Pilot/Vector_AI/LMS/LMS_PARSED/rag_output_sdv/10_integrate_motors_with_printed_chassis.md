## **Integrating Motors with Your 3D Printed Chassis**

Now that your custom-designed chassis has been 3D printed, it's time to bring your robot to life by integrating the motors and electronics!

This step is where design meets functionality — you’ll install both DC motors for driving and a servo motor for steering, attaching them securely to your printed frame.

### **DC Motor Integration (Driving)**


- 

The DC motor is responsible for making your robot move forward and backward.


- 

These motors will be attached to the rear section of your chassis, where your differential gear system connects to the wheels.


- 

Make sure the motors are tightly fitted using screws, brackets, or glue, depending on your chassis design.


- 

Test alignment: The axle and wheels must spin freely without rubbing or resistance.




*Tip: Be sure the motor ports are accessible so you can easily connect them to your controller later.*


### **Servo Motor Integration (Steering)**


- 

The servo motor controls the steering mechanism at the front of the robot.


- 

It should be mounted in a secure slot or cavity you built into your chassis.


- 

The steering link from the servo connects to your 2-bar steering system to turn the wheels.


- 

Double-check that your servo has enough range to steer left and right — and that it returns to center when powered off.




*Tip: Mount your servo in a centered, level position so your robot doesn’t turn unevenly.*

## Challenge #19: Out and Back Mission

<!-- type: challenge -->

Assignment: Challenge #19: Out and Back Mission

**Objective:**
Design a program that makes your car drive straight for a certain distance, then return to the launch position accurately.

### **Challenge Details:**


- 

**Start Point:** Mark a "Launch Zone" using tape on the ground.


- 

**Drive Forward:** The car must drive forward in a straight line for 200 cm


- 

**Return:** After reaching the target distance, the car must stop, pause for 1 second, then reverse and return back to the exact launch zone.

Assignment: Challenge#20: Autonomous Lap Drive

**Objective:**
Your task is to program your self-driving robot car to complete one full lap of the mat layout autonomously, following the path given as accurately and efficiently as possible.

### **Challenge Details:**


- 

Your car must start from a designated Start Zone and complete a full loop of the track, returning to the start point.


- 

The robot must stay within the road boundaries throughout the lap.


- 

The course must be completed without any human control once the car is placed at the start line and activated.
