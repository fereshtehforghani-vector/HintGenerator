Class 1 : Introduction to Drive Train

### **What is a Steering Mechanism?**

A steering mechanism is the system in a car that changes the direction of the front wheels, allowing the vehicle to turn left or right. Without steering, the car would only move in a straight line.

### **Real-Life Example:**

Imagine you're riding a bicycle. When you turn the handlebars, the front wheel turns in the direction you want to go — left or right. A car works similarly, but with two front wheels that must turn together and at the right angle.

### **Why Can’t All Wheels Turn?**

Only the front wheels turn because they’re responsible for steering. The rear wheels follow the direction set by the front.

### **Ackermann Steering Geometry**

In most cars, the front wheels don't turn at the same angle. When turning, the inner wheel needs to turn sharper than the outer wheel because it's following a smaller circle.

This is called Ackermann Steering.

#### Visualize it like this:

If your car is turning left:


- 

The left wheel (inside the turn) turns more.


- 

The right wheel (outside the turn) turns a little.



This makes the car turn smoothly without slipping or dragging.

## Challenge #01: Steering Mechanism

<!-- type: challenge -->

Assignment: Challenge #01: Steering Mechanism

**Objective:**
Design and build a functional steering mechanism using LEGO Technic pieces that mimics the basic principles of how a real car steering works.

**Goal:**
Create a mechanism that allows the steering wheel or gear to turn the front wheels left and right in a smooth and controlled manner.

### **Requirements:**


- 

Use LEGO Technic beams, gears, axles, and connectors.


- 

Your design should include:


  - 

A steering input (wheel or gear).


  - 

A linkage or gear system that turns both wheels together.


  - 

A fixed chassis or base to support the mechanism.




- 

The mechanism should not fall apart when used.


- 

The steering must be responsive—wheels should turn clearly and proportionally when input is applied.



### **Things to Think About:**


- 

How do cars make the front wheels turn left or right?


- 

What kind of connection is needed between the steering wheel and the wheels?


- 

Should the wheels be on an axle that rotates together or separately?



### **Hints:**


- 

Try to use rack and pinion, or simple gear and beam setups.


- 

Test your mechanism often—minor tweaks can make a big difference!


- 

Keep it compact and stable.

## Challenge #02: Robot Chassis

<!-- type: challenge -->

Assignment: Challenge #02: Robot Chassis

**Objective:**
Design and construct a strong, stable LEGO robot chassis that includes the steering mechanism you built in Challenge #1.

**Goal:**
Your final build should look and function like the frame of a small robotic car with a working front-wheel steering system.

### **Requirements:**


- 

Use LEGO Technic parts to build a complete chassis (base frame) for a robot vehicle.


- 

Attach your working steering mechanism from Challenge #1 to the front of the chassis.


- 

The chassis should:


  - 

Support four wheels (2 front, two back).


  - 

Be sturdy and well-balanced.


  - 

Allow space for adding motors, sensors, and other components later.




- 

You must be able to push the chassis manually and see the steering working clearly.



### **Things to Think About:**


- 

How do real vehicles support all four wheels?


- 

How will you keep your steering aligned with the chassis?


- 

Where should the motor or battery go in the future?


- 

Are the axles and wheels level and aligned?



### **Hints:**


- 

Reinforce your structure with beams and connectors to avoid wobbly wheels.


- 

Make sure the wheels don’t rub against any other parts.


- 

Think modular — it will be easier to add electronics later.
