## Class 4: Variables Intro & Camel Case

<!-- type: lesson -->

Class 4: Variables Intro & Camel Case

 

![moving boxes](https://dp4rp4tcpuoo1.cloudfront.net/R400V2/vbox2.png)

Imagine you're managing a **big storage room** filled with important items—books, toys, and plates. To keep everything organized, you use **labeled boxes** to store each type of item separately. In **Scratch Word Blocks for SPIKE**, variables work just like these boxes! They **hold information** and can **change** whenever needed.

**CamelCase:**![camel](https://dp4rp4tcpuoo1.cloudfront.net/R400V2/camel.png)

CamelCase is a way to name variables in coding that makes them **easy to read**. Imagine a camel 🐫—it has **humps**, and in CamelCase, words are joined together with **humps** (capital letters) to make them stand out!

Here are **simple rules** for using CamelCase when naming variables:

1️⃣ **No Spaces** – Join words together without spaces.


- ✅ `myVariable`


- ❌ `my variable`



2️⃣ **Start with a Lowercase Letter** – The first letter is always lowercase, but each new word starts with a capital letter.


- ✅ `robotSpeed`


- ❌ `RobotSpeed`



3️⃣ **Use Capital Letters for New Words** – This makes the name easy to read.


- ✅ `gameScore`


- ❌ `gamescore`



4️⃣ **Keep It Short but Clear** – Use names that describe what the variable does.


- ✅ `playerScore`


- ❌ `x`



5️⃣ **No Special Characters** – Use only letters and numbers (but don’t start with a number).

**Variables in the real world:**

![moving boxes](https://dp4rp4tcpuoo1.cloudfront.net/R400V2/vgame.png)

### **How Do Video Games Use Variables?**

Think about your favorite video game. How does it **remember your score**? How does it **track your health, coins, or level progress**?

✔️ When you **collect a coin**, the **Coins variable** goes up!
✔️ When you **lose a life**, the **Lives variable** goes down!
✔️ When you **win a level**, the **Level variable** increases!

Without variables, games wouldn't know **how many points you’ve scored** or **when you’ve won**. Every time you see numbers changing in a game, **a variable is working behind the scenes!**
