**  Course Learning Outcomes**

By the end of the **Reactive Robots 2.0** course, students will be able to:


- 
**Control Structures:** Utilize control blocks and loops to create efficient and structured robot behaviors.

- 
**Parallel Programming:** Implement concurrent execution of tasks, allowing the robot to handle multiple actions simultaneously.

- 
**Functions & Variables:** Organize and store data effectively to improve program efficiency and modularity.

- 
**Force Sensor:** Use force sensor inputs to trigger actions, enabling responsive and interactive robot designs.

- 
**Beginning Passive Attachments:** Design and implement basic mechanical components such as bumpers, wedges, plows, hooks, and forks for physical interactions.

## Intro to Reactive Robots

<!-- type: lesson -->

Intro to Reactive Robots

### Welcome to **Reactive Robots 2.0**


In our first course, your code followed a simple path—running from top to bottom, one block at a time. But now, it’s time to take things to the next level. Instead of executing commands in a straight line, you’ll learn **new ways to control how your robot runs its code**.

A big part of this course will be **working with the yellow Control & Event blocks**.

![code](https://dp4rp4tcpuoo1.cloudfront.net/R400V2/ceb.png)

These blocks are the key to making your robot’s actions more flexible, allowing it to **repeat, pause, and even multitask**.

Here’s what you’ll explore:


1. 
**Loops** – Make your robot repeat actions without rewriting the same code over and over.

2. 
**Parallel Programming** – Run multiple code stacks at the same time for more advanced behaviors.

3. 
**Wait Blocks** – Pause your code at just the right moment for better timing and reactions.


![code](https://dp4rp4tcpuoo1.cloudfront.net/R400V2/mbv.png)

**4. Variables** – Store and use information to make your robot smarter.
**5.** **Functions** – Create reusable blocks of code to simplify your programs.

We’ll also introduce **two new sensors** to help your robot interact with its environment:

![code](https://dp4rp4tcpuoo1.cloudfront.net/R400V2/fus.png)
 

**6.  Force Sensor** – Detects how much pressure is being applied, allowing your robot to respond to touch.

**7.  Ultrasonic Sensor** – Measures distance using sound waves, helping your robot detect and avoid obstacles.

By the end of this course, you’ll have a **strong understanding of how to control the flow of your programs**, making your robot more **responsive and dynamic**. Let’s get started.
