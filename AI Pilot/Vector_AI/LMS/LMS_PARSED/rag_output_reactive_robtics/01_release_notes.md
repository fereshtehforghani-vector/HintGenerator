Class 0: Notes

### **Coach’s Notes – Reactive Robots 2.0**

At this stage, students should have **strong mastery of basic movement**. This means that **"close enough" is not acceptable**—we are now expecting precise execution of turns and movement. Students must **fully complete each task as instructed**, reinforcing accuracy and control in their programming.

### **Coaching Tips & Encouragement**


1. 

**Plan Before Executing**


  - Students should **strategize** before diving into challenges.

  - Encourage the use of **pseudocode and mission pathing** to break down the task before coding.

  - A clear plan leads to more efficient problem-solving.



2. 

**Use Comments & Sensory Feedback**


  - 
**Commenting code** helps with understanding and debugging.

  - 
**Auditory (beeps) and visual (LED changes) cues** can make troubleshooting easier.



3. 

**Design Attachments with Flexibility**


  - Remind students that **robots are rarely perfect**—attachments should allow for slight variations in positioning.

  - A well-designed attachment can **compensate for small errors** in movement.



4. 

**Troubleshoot Step by Step**


  - Instead of guessing what’s wrong, students should **break the code into chunks** and test piece by piece.

  - Encourage them to **start at the beginning and add lines gradually** to pinpoint failures.

  - This structured approach saves time compared to trial-and-error guessing.



5. 

**Meet All Challenge Requirements**


  - Ensure students are **following every rule as written**—no skipping steps!

  - If their solution doesn’t fully meet the criteria, they **need to refine and correct it** before moving on.




By reinforcing these habits, students will **develop stronger problem-solving skills, cleaner code, and more reliable robots**.

Hello and welcome to **Robotics Engineer I: Reactive Robots 2.0 !**

In this **intermediate-level LEGO SPIKE Prime course**, you'll take your robotics skills to the next level by designing and programming robots that **react** to their surroundings. Building on fundamental concepts, you’ll learn how to integrate sensors, create interactive behaviors, and develop more advanced movement strategies.

By the end of this course, you’ll not only refine your programming and building skills but also gain the confidence to create robots that think and respond dynamically to the world around them.

Let’s get started on this exciting journey into **Reactive Robots**

**  Course Learning Outcomes**

By the end of the **Reactive Robots 2.0** course, students will be able to:


- 
**Control Structures:** Utilize control blocks and loops to create efficient and structured robot behaviors.

- 
**Parallel Programming:** Implement concurrent execution of tasks, allowing the robot to handle multiple actions simultaneously.

- 
**Functions & Variables:** Organize and store data effectively to improve program efficiency and modularity.

- 
**Force Sensor:** Use force sensor inputs to trigger actions, enabling responsive and interactive robot designs.

- 
**Beginning Passive Attachments:** Design and implement basic mechanical components such as bumpers, wedges, plows, hooks, and forks for physical interactions.

- 
**Intermediate Passive Attachments:** Integrate more advanced mechanisms like one-way doors and carabiners to enhance controlled interactions.

- 
**Ultrasonic Sensor:** Program the ultrasonic sensor for distance detection, enabling navigation and obstacle avoidance.


Through hands-on challenges and guided problem-solving, students will refine their **robotic engineering** and **programming** skills, preparing them for more complex robotics projects.
